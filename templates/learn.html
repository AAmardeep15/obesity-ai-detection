Complete Guide to Obesity

What Is Obesity?

Obesity is a chronic medical condition in which excess body fat increases the risk of serious health problems. It is not only a cosmetic concern; it is a metabolic and cardiovascular risk condition.

BMI = Weight (kg) / Height (m)^2

BMI is a screening tool. Clinical decisions should also consider waist circumference, body composition, blood markers, family history, and lifestyle.

Main Causes and Risk Factors

Diet

Frequent ultra-processed foods, sugary drinks, and oversized portions.

Inactivity

Low daily movement, prolonged sitting, and irregular exercise.

Genetics

Family history and hormone-regulation differences can increase risk.

Sleep & Stress

Sleep deprivation and chronic stress alter appetite control and behavior.

Complications You Should Know

  • Type 2 diabetes, insulin resistance, and fatty liver disease
  • High blood pressure, heart disease, and stroke risk
  • Sleep apnea, joint pain, and reduced physical capacity
  • Hormonal disorders and fertility problems
  • Anxiety, low self-esteem, and reduced quality of life

How To Cure / Manage Obesity (Evidence-Based)

In clinical practice, obesity is usually managed, not "instantly cured." Sustainable progress comes from consistent nutrition, exercise, sleep, and medical guidance.

Nutrition Therapy

Create a calorie deficit with balanced protein, fiber, and hydration.

Exercise Plan

Combine cardio and strength training; progress gradually and safely.

Behavior Change

Meal timing, stress control, sleep hygiene, and habit tracking.

Medical Care

For severe obesity, seek physician-guided medications or specialist care.

Prevention Strategy

  • Prefer whole foods over packaged foods
  • Control portion size and avoid sugary drinks
  • Target 150 to 300 minutes activity per week
  • Sleep at least 7 hours consistently
  • Track weight, waist, and food intake weekly
  • Reduce stress with breathing or mindfulness
  • Build family support and healthy routines
  • Consult professionals early if weight rises steadily
